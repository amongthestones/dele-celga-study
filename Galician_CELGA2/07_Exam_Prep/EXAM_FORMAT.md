# CELGA 2 — Exam Format
## Estrutura da proba

---

## Overview

CELGA 2 certifies CEFR **B1** Galician and is run by the **Secretaría Xeral da Lingua (Xunta de Galicia)**. The exam has two parts on the same day: a **written test** and an **oral test**.

> ⚠️ **Always confirm the current format and cut scores** in the official *convocatoria* (the DOG resolution published each year) and the sample exams at **lingua.gal**. Task counts and weightings are adjusted from time to time. The outline below reflects the standard recent structure.

---

## Part 1: Written Test (Proba escrita) — max. ~2 hours

The written test is organised in several blocks. In recent convocatorias these are:

| Block | What it tests | Notes |
|-------|---------------|-------|
| **Comprensión oral** | Listening to a recorded text and answering questions | You may take notes (~10 min with the source) |
| **Comprensión escrita** | Reading one or more texts and answering questions | True/false, multiple choice, matching |
| **Estrutura da lingua e do texto** | Grammar and vocabulary in context | Gap-fill, word forms, contractions, verb forms |
| **Expresión escrita** | Producing one or two short texts (~150–200 words) | Email, letter, note, short informative/opinion text |

**Typical writing formats:** a formal or informal email/letter, a note or message, a short informative or narrative text. You're assessed on task completion, correctness (including **contractions, clitic placement, no Spanish interference**), range and cohesion.

---

## Part 2: Oral Test (Proba oral) — max. ~10 minutes, recorded

| Stage | Approx. time | Graded? |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| Welcome / personal contact | 1–2 min | No |
| Summary of a short text or prompt | ~2 min | Yes |
| Short monologue / account on a topic | ~3 min | Yes |
| Conversation / interaction with the examiners | ~4 min | Yes |

The whole oral is **recorded** as a guarantee of the process. You may get a couple of minutes to look at the prompt before speaking.

---

## Passing

CELGA results are reported as **APTO / NON APTO** (pass/fail). To pass you generally need to reach a minimum in the grouped skill areas **and** an overall minimum.

> The exact minimum percentages are set in each year's convocatoria and the correction criteria — **check the official document for the year you sit the exam** rather than relying on a fixed number. Plan to be comfortably above any threshold in every block, since a single very weak skill can fail you.

---

## Registration & Logistics

- **Where to register:** sede.xunta.gal (procedure code **PL500C**) and information at **lingua.gal**.
- **When:** convened, in recent years, around **twice a year** (commonly spring and autumn), published in the **DOG** (Diario Oficial de Galicia).
- **Where you can sit it:** typically Santiago de Compostela and additional centres (e.g. Madrid, Ponferrada) — confirm in the convocatoria.
- **Cost:** there is an exam fee set in the convocatoria.
