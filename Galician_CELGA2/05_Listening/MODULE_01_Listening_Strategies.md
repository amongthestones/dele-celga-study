# Module 1: Listening Strategies for CELGA 2
## Comprensión oral

---

## Overview

In CELGA 2 listening (*comprensión oral*) you hear a recorded text — a conversation, an announcement, a short talk or interview — and answer questions on the written test. You can usually take notes. The voices are **authentic Galician**, so you'll meet real accents, **gheada** and **seseo**.

> Confirm the current number of audios, plays and items in the sample exams at **lingua.gal**.

---

## Part 1: What the Listening Tests

- Main idea and specific details of everyday spoken texts
- Practical information: times, places, prices, instructions
- Who says what (in conversations/interviews)
- Speaker attitude/intention at a basic level

---

## Part 2: Core Strategies

### 1. Use the reading time
Read the questions **before** the audio. Underline what each one asks (a time? a reason? a place?). This tells your ear what to listen for.

### 2. Note like a telegram
Jot numbers, names, places and key verbs — not full sentences. Use symbols (→, +, ?, ≠).

### 3. Don't freeze on one word
If you miss something, let it go and stay with the flow. A single unknown word rarely costs you the answer.

### 4. Expect gheada and seseo
You'll hear *gato* as "hato" (gheada) and *cinco* as "sinco" (seseo). These are **pronunciations only** — the word and its meaning are standard. Train your ear so they don't startle you.

### 5. Watch the Galician/Spanish sound shifts
Recognizing the regular correspondences helps you catch words fast:

| Spanish sound | Galician | Example |
|---------------|----------|---------|
| -ción | **-ción / -zón** | canción → canción |
| j / ge,gi | **x** | hijo → fillo, gente → xente |
| ll | **ll / ch** | lleno → cheo |
| -n- (lost) | (kept/changed) | luna → lúa |
| ó/ue | **o** | puerta → porta, bueno → bo |

### 6. Numbers, dates and times
Drill these — they're the most common "specific detail" questions. Galician *dezaseis, vinte e dous, ás once e media, o día doce*.

---

## Part 3: Listening Text Types

- **Conversas** (everyday dialogues): plans, shopping, arranging things
- **Avisos e anuncios** (announcements): stations, shops, public notices
- **Entrevistas** (interviews): someone answering about their life/work
- **Monólogos curtos** (short talks): explaining or recommending something

---

## Part 4: Build the Habit (free, authentic Galician)

A little every day trains the ear faster than long rare sessions:

- **Radio Galega** (crtvg.es/rg) — news and talk, native speed
- **TVG / TVG Play** (crtvg.es) — news, series, documentaries; subtitles often available
- **Ben Falado!** (CRTVG) — short clips on usage and common mistakes
- **Podcasts** from CRTVG and Galician creators

Mix **active** listening (15 min with notes, then summarize aloud in Galician) with **passive** listening (radio in the background).
