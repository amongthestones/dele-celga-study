# Module 1: Reading Strategies for CELGA 2
## Comprensión escrita

---

## Overview

CELGA 2 reading (*comprensión escrita*) gives you one or more short texts — a notice, an email, a news item, a short narrative — and asks true/false, multiple-choice or matching questions. The texts are **everyday B1**: nothing literary or technical. Your job is to read efficiently and not be tricked by Spanish look-alikes.

> Confirm the current task types and number of items in the sample exams at **lingua.gal**.

---

## Part 1: What the Reading Tests

- Main idea and specific details of a practical text
- Understanding instructions, notices and correspondence
- Vocabulary in context (often where Galician differs from Spanish)
- Simple inference (what the writer wants, how they feel)

---

## Part 2: Core Strategies

### 1. Read the questions first
Know what you're hunting for before you read the text. Underline the key word in each question.

### 2. Skim, then scan
First pass for the gist (what kind of text, what's it about). Second pass to locate the exact line that answers each question.

### 3. Don't stop at unknown words
Galician has many words that *look* unfamiliar but are guessable from context or from Spanish/Portuguese. Keep going; meaning usually clarifies.

### 4. Beware false friends and look-alikes
This is where CELGA reading catches Spanish speakers. A distractor will often use a word that *looks* right but means something else. Examples to keep in mind:

| Galician | Means | NOT |
|----------|-------|-----|
| **vasoira** | broom | (not "vaso") |
| **embarazada** | pregnant | (same trap as Spanish) |
| **constipado** | having a cold | (not constipated) |
| **bágoa** | tear (crying) | — |
| **rúa** | street | — |
| **agasallo** | gift | — |
| **billa** | tap/faucet | (not "billete") |
| **cadeira** | chair | (not "cadera"=hip) |

### 5. Verify against the text
For true/false especially: find the exact sentence that proves your answer. If you can't find it, it's probably false or "not stated".

---

## Part 3: Reading Text Types You'll Meet

- **Avisos e anuncios** (notices, ads): opening hours, rules, offers
- **Correspondencia** (emails, letters, messages): requests, invitations, complaints
- **Noticias breves** (short news): a local event, a piece of information
- **Textos informativos** (leaflets, instructions): how something works
- **Narracións curtas** (short personal narratives): someone telling a story

---

## Part 4: Question Types and Tactics

| Question type | Tactic |
|---------------|--------|
| **Verdadeiro / Falso** | Find the exact supporting line; "not mentioned" usually = false |
| **Opción múltiple** | Eliminate options that use text words but twist the meaning |
| **Relacionar** (matching) | Match the easy/obvious ones first, then use elimination |
| **Ocos** (gap-fill) | Read the whole sentence; check grammar (contraction? verb form?) and meaning together |

---

## Part 5: Build the Habit

Fifteen minutes of real Galician a day beats any single tactic. Rotate the registers the exam pulls from:

- **News:** praza.gal, Nós Diario (nosdiario.gal), GCiencia
- **Culture / stories:** culturagalega.gal, short pieces by Agustín Fernández Paz, Manuel Rivas
- **Practical text:** read the Galician version of signs, menus, council notices

Keep a small notebook of words where Galician differs from Spanish — that list *is* your exam-vocabulary list.

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Read and answer

> **AVISO**
> A biblioteca municipal permanecerá pechada o vindeiro luns 12 por mantemento. O martes retomarase o horario habitual (de 9.00 a 21.00 h). Lembrámoslles ás persoas usuarias que poden renovar os préstamos a través da páxina web. Desculpen as molestias.

1. (V/F) A biblioteca estará pechada o martes.
2. (V/F) Pódense renovar os préstamos por internet.
3. A que hora pecha a biblioteca un día normal?

### Exercise 2: Guess from context

What does the underlined word mean?
1. "Colleu a **vasoira** e varreu a cociña." → __________
2. "Está **constipado**: ten tose e a nariz tapada." → __________

---

## Answer Key

### Exercise 1:
1. Falso (estará pechada o **luns**; o martes abre normal).
2. Verdadeiro ("poden renovar os préstamos a través da páxina web").
3. Pecha ás 21.00 h.

### Exercise 2:
1. broom (she swept the kitchen)
2. has a cold (cough, blocked nose)
