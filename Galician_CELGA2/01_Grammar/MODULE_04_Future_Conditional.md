# Module 4: Future and Conditional in Galician
## Futuro e Condicional

---

## Overview

The Galician future and conditional are built on the same principle as Spanish: take the **whole infinitive** and add one fixed set of endings. The endings are *not* the same as Spanish, so watch them closely.

**Prerequisites:** Modules 1–3 (articles, present, past). A note on Galician having **no compound tenses** is covered in Module 6 — it matters here, because Galician does not use "haber + participle" the way Spanish does.

---

## Part 1: The Future (Futuro)

### Formation

Infinitive + these endings (the same for **-ar, -er, -ir**):

| Person | Ending | falar | comer | vivir |
|--------|--------|-------|-------|-------|
| eu | **-ei** | falarei | comerei | vivirei |
| ti | **-ás** | falarás | comerás | vivirás |
| el/ela/vostede | **-á** | falará | comerá | vivirá |
| nós | **-emos** | falaremos | comeremos | viviremos |
| vós | **-edes** | falaredes | comeredes | viviredes |
| eles/elas/vostedes | **-án** | falarán | comerán | vivirán |

> Note the differences from Spanish: **nós -emos** (Sp. *-emos* too, but Galician keeps the theme vowel: *falaremos*) and especially **vós -edes** (*falaredes*), where Spanish has *-éis* (*hablaréis*).

### Irregular future stems

A handful of verbs change the stem (the endings stay the same). The most frequent:

| Infinitive | Stem | Example |
|------------|------|---------|
| dicir | dir- | direi, dirás, dirá... |
| facer | far- | farei, farás, fará... |
| haber | haber-/hab- | haberá |
| poñer | poñer- (regular) | poñerei |
| ter | ter- (regular) | terei |
| vir | vir- (regular) | virei |

> Galician keeps far more **regular** future stems than Spanish. Spanish *pondré, tendré, vendré, saldré* correspond to regular Galician **poñerei, terei, virei, sairei**. Only **dicir → direi** and **facer → farei** are truly irregular and high-frequency.

### Uses

1. **Future actions:** *Mañá falaremos co director.* (Tomorrow we'll talk to the director.)
2. **Probability/conjecture in the present:** *Onde está Ana? — Estará na casa.* (She's probably at home.)
   - *Serán as tres.* (It must be about three.)
3. **The "going to" future** is usually expressed with **ir a + infinitive**, very common in speech: *Vou comprar pan.* (I'm going to buy bread.)

## Part 2: The Conditional (Condicional)

### Formation

Infinitive + these endings (same for all three conjugations):

| Person | Ending | falar | comer | vivir |
|--------|--------|-------|-------|-------|
| eu | **-ía** | falaría | comería | viviría |
| ti | **-ías** | falarías | comerías | vivirías |
| el/ela | **-ía** | falaría | comería | viviría |
| nós | **-iamos** | falariamos | comeriamos | viviriamos |
| vós | **-iades** | falariades | comeriades | viviriades |
| eles/elas | **-ían** | falarían | comerían | vivirían |

The **irregular stems are the same as the future** (dir-, far-): *diría, faría*.

### Uses

1. **Hypothetical situations:** *Gustaríame viaxar máis.* (I would like to travel more.)
2. **Polite requests:** *Poderías axudarme?* (Could you help me?) · *Quería un café, por favor.* (I'd like a coffee.)
3. **Probability in the past:** *Serían as dúas cando chegou.* (It was probably two when he arrived.)
4. **Future-in-the-past / reported speech:** *Dixo que viría.* (He said he would come.)

---

## Part 3: Conditional Sentences (Oracións Condicionais)

Galician condicionais mirror Spanish closely. Note: the imperfect subjunctive endings (covered in Module 6 context) are **-se / -ra** as in Spanish but with Galician spelling.

| Type | Se clause | Main clause | Example |
|------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Real | presente indicativo | futuro / presente / imperativo | *Se chove, non saímos.* |
| Hypothetical | imperfecto de subxuntivo | condicional | *Se tivese tempo, viaxaría.* |

> ⚠️ As in Spanish, **never** put a conditional after *se*. ❌ *Se tería tempo* → ✓ *Se tivese tempo*.

Because Galician has **no compound tenses**, the "impossible past" (Spanish *si hubiera sabido, habría venido*) is rendered with the pluperfect/imperfect subjunctive + conditional: *Se o souber / soubese, viría* or, in careful registers, with the **pluscuamperfecto de subxuntivo** *(soubese)* — see Module 6.

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Future

Conjugate in the future:
1. Eu _______ (falar) con ela mañá.
2. Nós _______ (comer) no restaurante.
3. Eles _______ (vivir) en Lugo.
4. Ti _______ (facer) os deberes?
5. Ela _______ (dicir) a verdade.
6. Vós _______ (ter) tempo?

### Exercise 2: Conditional

Conjugate in the conditional:
1. _______ (gustar) me viaxar a Galicia.
2. Ti _______ (poder) axudarme?
3. Eles _______ (vir) se os invitases.
4. Nós _______ (facer) o mesmo.
5. Eu _______ (dicir) que si.

### Exercise 3: Future or Conditional?

1. Onde está Xoán? — _______ (estar) no traballo. (probability now)
2. Dixo que _______ (chegar) ás cinco. (reported speech)
3. Mañá _______ (saír/nós) cedo. (plan)
4. _______ (querer) un café, por favor. (polite request)

### Exercise 4: Conditional Sentences

1. Se _______ (ter/eu) diñeiro, mercaría unha casa.
2. Se chove, _______ (quedar/nós) na casa.
3. Se _______ (poder/ti), virías?

## Common Errors

1. **Spanish vós ending:** ❌ *falaréis* → ✓ *falaredes*
2. **Over-irregularizing the stem:** ❌ *terei → tendría* → ✓ *terei / tería* (regular stem)
3. **Conditional after se:** ❌ *Se tería* → ✓ *Se tivese*
