## Overview

The present tense in Galician is similar to Spanish but with key differences in endings and some irregular forms. Your Spanish will help, but pay attention to the differences!

**Time to complete:** 3 weeks
**Prerequisites:** Module 1 (Articles)

## Part 1: Regular Verb Conjugations

### First Conjugation: -AR Verbs

**FALAR (to speak/talk)**

| Person | Conjugation | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| eu | fal**o** | Eu falo galego. |
| ti | fal**as** | Ti falas moi ben. |
| el/ela/vostede | fal**a** | Ela fala inglés. |
| nós | fal**amos** | Nós falamos galego. |
| vós | fal**ades** | Vós falades rápido. |
| eles/elas/vostedes | fal**an** | Eles falan español. |

**Common -AR verbs:**
- traballar (to work)
- estudar (to study)
- cantar (to sing)
- comprar (to buy)
- mirar (to look)
- chamar (to call)
- amar (to love)
- pasar (to pass/spend time)

## Part 3: Spanish vs Galician Comparison

| Spanish | Galician | Meaning |
|---------|----------|---------|
| tengo | teño | I have |
| hago | fago | I do/make |
| pongo | poño | I put |
| vengo | veño | I come |
| digo | digo | I say |
| sé | sei | I know |
| voy | vou | I go |
| doy | dou | I give |
| soy | son | I am |
| estoy | estou | I am |

**Key patterns to note:**
- Spanish "ng" → Galician "ñ": tengo → teño
- Spanish "hago" → Galician "fago" (h → f)
- Spanish endings often different for vosotros/vós

## Part 4: Uses of Present Tense

### 1. Current Actions and States
- *Estudo galego.* (I study Galician.)
- *Traballo nun banco.* (I work at a bank.)

### 2. Habitual Actions
- *Sempre tomo café pola mañá.* (I always have coffee in the morning.)
- *Os domingos vou ao fútbol.* (On Sundays I go to football.)

### 3. General Truths
- *A auga ferve aos 100 graos.* (Water boils at 100 degrees.)
- *O sol sae polo leste.* (The sun rises in the east.)

### 4. Scheduled Future
- *Mañá chega o meu irmán.* (Tomorrow my brother arrives.)
- *O curso comeza en setembro.* (The course begins in September.)

### 5. Historical Present (narrative)
- *En 1492, Colón chega a América.* (In 1492, Columbus arrives in America.)

## Part 5: Forming Questions and Negatives

### Questions
- Invert or use question intonation:
  - *Falas galego?* (Do you speak Galician?)
  - *Onde vives?* (Where do you live?)
  - *Que fas?* (What are you doing?)

### Negatives
- Place "non" before the verb:
  - *Non falo francés.* (I don't speak French.)
  - *Non teño tempo.* (I don't have time.)
  - *Non sei.* (I don't know.)

## Common Errors

1. **Using Spanish endings:**
   - ❌ Vosotros habláis → ✓ Vós falades

2. **Forgetting the ñ:**
   - ❌ Eu teno → ✓ Eu teño

3. **Using "hacer" instead of "facer":**
   - ❌ Eu hago → ✓ Eu fago

4. **Wrong "vós" ending:**
   - ❌ Vós comeis → ✓ Vós comedes
