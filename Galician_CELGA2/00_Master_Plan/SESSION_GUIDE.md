# CELGA 2 — Session Guide
## 2 sessions per week | Saturday + Sunday | 60 min each

Open this file. Find your session. Do it.

---

## Weekly Structure

| Day | Session | Duration |
|-----|---------|----------|
| Saturday | Grammar + Writing | 60 min |
| Sunday | Listening + Speaking | 60 min |

Each session type rotates on a 4-week cycle. Find your current week number (Week 1, 2, 3, or 4) and do that session. After Week 4, restart at Week 1.

---

## SATURDAY SESSIONS — Grammar + Writing (60 min)

### Saturday Week 1 — Grammar Focus

**What you need:** One grammar module from `01_Grammar/`

**Steps:**
1. Open the next grammar module you haven't completed (start with MODULE_01, work in order)
2. Read the explanation section (15 min)
3. Do all the exercises in the module (30 min)
4. Write 5 original sentences using the grammar point you just studied (10 min)
5. Mark any sentences you're unsure about — bring them to a native speaker or check with a dictionary

**Done when:** Exercises complete, 5 sentences written.

---

### Saturday Week 2 — Writing Practice

**What you need:** Writing prompt from the list below, timer

**Steps:**
1. Pick the next prompt from the Writing Prompts section below (work in order)
2. Set a timer for **40 minutes**
3. Write your response entirely in Galician. Do not switch to Spanish.
4. When the timer ends, re-read your text and mark every word you wrote in Spanish by mistake
5. Replace each Spanish word with the Galician equivalent (use academia.gal if needed)

**Done when:** Text written, all Spanish interference words identified and replaced.

---

### Saturday Week 3 — Vocabulary: Interference Words

**What you need:** Interference vocabulary list below, Anki

**Steps:**
1. Open the Interference Vocabulary section below
2. Study the next 15 words in the list (work in order)
3. Add all 15 to Anki: front = Spanish word, back = Galician equivalent + example sentence
4. Do one Anki review (new cards only, ~15 min)
5. Write 3 sentences using words from the set you just studied

**Done when:** 15 words in Anki, 3 sentences written.

---

### Saturday Week 4 — Reading + Text Analysis

**What you need:** A Galician news article

**Steps:**
1. Go to **praza.gal** or **galiciaconfidencial.com**
2. Find an article of at least 400 words. Read it once (10 min). Note any unknown words.
3. Write a 5-bullet summary of the article in Galician (10 min)
4. Find 5 expressions or words in the article you wouldn't have written yourself. Add to Anki (10 min)
5. Rewrite one paragraph from the article in your own words (10 min)
6. Check your rewrite against the original: where did you use Spanish words or structures? (10 min)

**Done when:** Summary written, words added, paragraph rewritten.

---

## SUNDAY SESSIONS — Listening + Speaking (60 min)

### Sunday Week 1 — TVG Listening

**What you need:** TVG (free online at tvg.gal or on TV)

**Steps:**
1. Choose any news program, documentary, or talk show on TVG (~25 min of viewing)
2. While watching: note 5 words or expressions you didn't understand
3. Look up those 5 words. Add them to Anki. (10 min)
4. Write 5 sentences in Galician summarizing what you watched (10 min)
5. Say the summary out loud. Record yourself if possible (5 min)

**Done when:** 5 words in Anki, summary written and spoken.

---

### Sunday Week 2 — Radio Galega Podcast

**What you need:** Radio Galega podcast (available on podcast apps or radiogalega.gal)

**Steps:**
1. Find any podcast episode from Radio Galega (~20 min)
2. Listen once without stopping. Take brief notes: topic, 3 key points.
3. Listen a second time and fill in gaps in your notes (15 min)
4. Write a short paragraph (80–100 words) in Galician summarizing the episode (10 min)
5. Compare your paragraph to your notes: did you miss anything important?

**Done when:** Notes complete, summary paragraph written.

---

### Sunday Week 3 — Speaking Practice

**What you need:** Timer, recording app (phone is fine)

**Steps:**
1. Choose a topic from the list below
2. Set a timer for **2 minutes**
3. Speak continuously on the topic in Galician. If you can't find a word, describe around it — don't switch to Spanish.
4. Record yourself
5. Listen back: note every time you switched to Spanish or stopped. Count them.
6. Try again with the same topic. Did the count go down?

**Topics (rotate through in order):**
1. Describe your neighborhood
2. Your routine on a typical weekday
3. A cultural tradition in Galicia you value
4. Your opinion on a current news story
5. The advantages and disadvantages of living in a small city vs a large city
6. A book, film, or show you've recently experienced
7. The relationship between Galician and Spanish in daily life
8. Something you'd change about your city or town
9. Your experience of learning Galician
10. What makes Galicia distinctive as a place

**Done when:** Two recordings made, interference points noted.

---

### Sunday Week 4 — Conversation in the Wild

**What you need:** An opportunity to speak Galician with a real person

This week, your session is not at home. The task:

**Use Galician in at least one real interaction this week.** This could be:
- Shopping in a local store
- A conversation with a neighbor
- A phone call to a local business or institution
- Texting a Galician-speaking friend entirely in Galician
- Attending a local cultural event where Galician is used

After the interaction, write 3–5 sentences about what happened, what you said, and where you struggled.

If no opportunity arises: do Sunday Week 3 instead.

**Done when:** Interaction happened, reflection written.

## Writing Prompts (Saturday Week 2 — work through in order)

### 1. [ ] Presentación persoal
Write a personal introduction (100 words) as if you were introducing yourself to a new group: where you're from, what you do, and one thing that makes your daily life in Galicia interesting.

### 2. [ ] Carta a un amigo
Write a friendly letter (100–120 words) to a Galician-speaking friend you haven't seen in a while. Tell them about something you've been doing recently and ask about their life.

### 3. [ ] O meu lugar favorito en Galicia
Describe your favorite place in Galicia (100–120 words). What is it? Why do you like it? What do you do there?

### 4. [ ] Opinión sobre o transporte público
Write a short opinion text (120 words) about public transport in your city or area. Is it good? What would you improve?

### 5. [ ] Carta formal ao concello
Write a formal letter (120–150 words) to your local town hall requesting an improvement to a public space or service in your neighborhood.

### 6. [ ] Comparar dous lugares
Compare two places you know well (120 words) — two cities, two neighborhoods, or two different environments. What are the differences?

### 7. [ ] Unha tradición galega
Describe a Galician cultural tradition (120 words). What is it? What does it mean to people? Have you participated in it?

### 8. [ ] O tempo libre
Write about how you spend your free time (100–120 words). What do you enjoy? Has this changed in recent years?

### 9. [ ] Carta de reclamación
Write a formal complaint letter (120–150 words) to a business or service that disappointed you. Be specific about the problem and what you want done.

### 10. [ ] Vantaxes e inconvenientes
Write a balanced opinion text (130–150 words) on the advantages and disadvantages of one of these:
- Living in a city vs a village
- Working from home vs in an office
- Learning a minority language vs focusing on major languages

### 11. [ ] Un evento cultural
Describe a cultural event you attended or would like to attend (120 words). What happens? Who goes? Why is it worth attending?

### 12. [ ] O galego no meu día a día
Write about the role Galician plays (or doesn't play) in your daily life (120–150 words). Where do you hear it? Where do you use it? What do you think about its situation?
