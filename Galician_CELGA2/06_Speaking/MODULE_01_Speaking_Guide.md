# Module 1: Speaking Guide for CELGA 2
## Proba oral

---

## Overview

The CELGA 2 oral is short (**max. ~10 minutes**) and **recorded**. After a brief, ungraded welcome chat, you summarize a short prompt, give a short monologue on a topic, and have a conversation with the examiners. You're assessed on getting your meaning across in **comprehensible, recognizably Galician** speech — not on sounding native.

> Confirm the current oral structure in the sample exams at **lingua.gal**.

---

## Part 1: The Stages

| Stage | ~time | Graded? | What to do |
|-------|-------|---------|------------|
| Welcome / personal contact | 1–2 min | No | Relax, answer simple questions (name, where you're from) |
| Summary of a text/prompt | ~2 min | Yes | Say the main idea and a couple of details in your own words |
| Short monologue | ~3 min | Yes | Talk about a topic: structure it, give examples, give an opinion |
| Conversation/interaction | ~4 min | Yes | Answer, react, ask back, keep it going |

---

## Part 2: What Examiners Reward

- **Fluency** over perfection — keep going, don't stall on one word
- **Galician forms**, not Spanish: *grazas*, *moi*, *entón*, enclitic pronouns (*gústame*, *paréceme*)
- **Range**: connectors, a few tenses, some opinion language
- **Interaction**: actually responding and asking, not reciting a script

> Biggest avoidable loss: switching into Spanish words/structures under pressure. Drill the high-frequency Galician forms until they're automatic.

---

## Part 3: Phrase Toolkit

**Buying time / starting:**
- A ver… / Pois… / Déixame pensar un momento.
- O tema do que vou falar é…

**Giving opinion:**
- Na miña opinión… / Ao meu ver… / Coido que… / Paréceme que…
- Estou de acordo / Non estou de acordo con iso porque…

**Structuring:**
- Primeiro… / Despois… / Ademais… / Por último…
- Por unha banda… por outra banda…

**Summarizing a text:**
- O texto fala de… / A idea principal é que… / Tamén menciona que…

**Interacting:**
- E ti que pensas? / Non si? / Pódesme repetir a pregunta, por favor?
- Si, exacto. / Claro. / Depende…

**Examples / reasons:**
- Por exemplo… / Como cando… / Porque… / Xa que…

---

## Part 4: Common Speaking Topics

- A miña familia e a miña rutina
- O meu traballo / os meus estudos
- O meu pobo ou cidade; Galicia
- As miñas afeccións e o tempo libre
- Unha viaxe ou unha experiencia recente
- A comida e as festas galegas
- A tecnoloxía na vida diaria
- O medio ambiente e o reciclaxe

---

## Part 5: How to Build a 3-minute Monologue

1. **Intro (15–20 s):** state the topic and your angle.
2. **Body (2 min):** 2–3 points, each with a reason or example. Use connectors.
3. **Close (20–30 s):** a short opinion or conclusion.

Mini-frame:
> *Vou falar de… Na miña opinión, hai varias cousas importantes. Primeiro,… Por exemplo,… Ademais,… Por último,… En definitiva, coido que…*

---

## Part 6: Build Fluency

- **Record yourself** for 2–3 minutes on a topic from Part 4; listen back; note 3 things to fix.
- **Shadow** Radio Galega / TVG: repeat along to copy rhythm and Galician sounds.
- **Language exchange / classes:** Tandem, HelloTalk, local *cursos de galego*, *italki*.
- **Think in Galician** during routine moments (commuting, cooking) to make the forms automatic.

---

## Part 7: Self-assessment (after each practice)

Rate 1–5:
- [ ] I kept going without long pauses
- [ ] I used Galician forms (no Spanish slips)
- [ ] I structured the monologue (intro–body–close)
- [ ] I reacted and asked back in the conversation
