# Language Exam Study Guide
## DELE B2 (Spanish) + CELGA 2 (Galician)

A self-study guide covering grammar, vocabulary, the four skills, and exam-specific strategy for two language certifications:

- **DELE B2** — Diploma de Español como Lengua Extranjera, upper-intermediate Spanish (Instituto Cervantes). CEFR **B2**.
- **CELGA 2** — Certificado de Lingua Galega (Xunta de Galicia). CEFR **B1**.

These are study materials, not a fixed schedule. Work through modules at whatever pace fits. Each file stands on its own, and answer keys are included throughout.

> **Who this is for:** independent learners — typically Spanish speakers (or strong Spanish learners) preparing CELGA 2 alongside upper-intermediate Spanish. The Galician modules lean on Spanish as a scaffold but flag every place the two languages diverge.

> **Accuracy & currency:** content is checked against authoritative sources (Instituto Cervantes / CVC for Spanish; RAG and the Xunta's Portal da Lingua for Galician). **Exam formats and pass marks change** — each exam-format file points you to the official source to confirm before you register. See the `EXAM_FORMAT.md` in each `07_Exam_Prep/` folder.

### DELE B2 exam at a glance

| Prueba | Skill | Time | Tasks |
|--------|-------|------|-------|
| 1 | Comprensión de lectura | 70 min | 4 (36 items) |
| 2 | Comprensión auditiva | 40 min | 5 (30 items) |
| 3 | Expresión e interacción escritas | 80 min | 2 (~150–180 words each) |
| 4 | Expresión e interacción orales | ~15–20 min + prep | 3 |

Pruebas are grouped: **Lectura+Escritura** and **Auditiva+Oral**. You need **≥60% in each group** to pass. Full detail in `Spanish_DELE_B2/07_Exam_Prep/EXAM_FORMAT.md`.

### CELGA 2 exam at a glance

- **Proba escrita** (max. ~2 h): blocks for *comprensión oral*, *comprensión escrita*, *estrutura da lingua e do texto*, and *expresión escrita* (one or two short texts, ~150–200 words).
- **Proba oral** (max. ~10 min, recorded): summary + short monologue + conversation.
- Result is reported **APTO / NON APTO**; you must reach the minimum set in that year's *convocatoria* in the grouped skills and overall.

Register at **sede.xunta.gal** (procedure **PL500C**); info and sample exams at **lingua.gal**. Full detail in `Galician_CELGA2/07_Exam_Prep/EXAM_FORMAT.md`.

## Suggested Order

If starting fresh, most learners benefit from:

1. **Diagnostic test** (`07_Exam_Prep/DIAGNOSTIC_TEST.md`) — find your weak areas.
2. **Read your exam's `EXAM_FORMAT.md`** so you know the target from day one.
3. **Grammar modules** in order — they build on each other.
4. **Vocabulary modules** alongside grammar, not after.
5. **Skill modules** (reading, writing, listening, speaking) once grammar basics are solid.
6. **Worksheets, prompt banks and past papers** in the weeks before the exam.

Cheatsheets are reference material — use them for quick review, not initial learning.

## A Note for Spanish Speakers Learning Galician

The Galician modules are written to exploit what you already know from Spanish while drilling the differences that trip people up: **article contractions** (do, na, polo, nun…), **clitic pronouns** (te vs che, and putting them *after* the verb), the **second form of the article**, the **personal infinitive**, and — above all — that **Galician has no compound tenses** (use *comín*, never *hei comido*). Watch for *castelanismos* in your own output: **moi** not *muy*, **grazas** not *gracias*, **entón** not *entonces*.
