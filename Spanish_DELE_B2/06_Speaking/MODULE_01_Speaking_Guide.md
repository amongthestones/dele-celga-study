# Module 1: DELE B2 Speaking Guide
## Oral Expression and Interaction

---

## Overview

DELE B2 *Expresión e Interacción Orales* is around 15 minutes of exam time plus 15 minutes of preparation. The test has three tasks, all involving interaction with the examiner. Confirm the current format at cervantes.es — the exam specification is updated periodically.

---

## Part 1: Exam Structure

| Task | Description | Time |
|------|-------------|------|
| 1 | Evaluate written proposals (monologue + conversation) | ~5–6 min |
| 2 | Describe a photograph and discuss it | ~3–4 min |
| 3 | Role-play / imagined situation related to the photograph | ~3–4 min |
| **Total exam** | | ~15 min |
| Preparation | You choose which options from Tasks 1 and 2 to work with | 15 min |

### Assessment Criteria

| Criterion | What's Evaluated |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Coherence | Logical organization, clear structure |
| Fluency | Smooth delivery, appropriate pace |
| Accuracy | Grammar and vocabulary correctness |
| Range | Variety of structures and vocabulary |
| Interaction | Turn-taking, reacting, negotiating |

## Part 2: Task 1 — Evaluating Proposals

### What You Receive
- A question or situation followed by a list of 6–8 written proposals (short options)
- You choose which proposals you would prioritize and justify your choice
- During preparation you decide your ordering and reasons

### Structure Your Response

**1. Introduction (15–30 seconds)**
- State the situation
- Announce your general criterion for choosing

**2. Argued selection (1–2 minutes)**
- Present the proposals you would prioritize, one by one
- Justify each one (why this helps, who benefits, what problem it solves)

**3. Rejection (30–45 seconds, optional)**
- Mention one or two proposals you would *not* choose and briefly explain why

**4. Conversation with examiner (2–3 minutes)**
- The examiner asks follow-up questions and may push back
- Elaborate, compare, defend your reasoning

### Useful Phrases — Task 1

**Introducing the situation:**
- El tema que se plantea es...
- Me gustaría comenzar por...
- Antes de nada, me centraré en...

**Justifying choices:**
- Opto por... porque me parece que...
- Lo considero prioritario ya que...
- Esta propuesta es interesante porque...
- Lo más eficaz sería... dado que...

**Ranking / ordering:**
- En primer lugar, elegiría...
- A continuación, destacaría...
- Por otro lado, también considero importante...

**Rejecting with reasons:**
- Descartaría... porque...
- No me parece la mejor opción, ya que...
- Esta propuesta no acaba de convencerme porque...

## Part 3: Task 2 — Describing a Photograph

### What You Receive
- One or two photographs of a situation involving people
- You describe what you see, infer context, and imagine what's happening

### Structure Your Description

**1. General description (30–45 seconds)**
- Where is the scene? Who appears in it?
- Overall atmosphere

**2. Detailed description (45–60 seconds)**
- Each person: what they're doing, how they seem to feel
- Setting details (time of day, weather, objects)

**3. Interpretation (45–60 seconds)**
- What might have happened before this moment?
- What do you imagine happens next?
- What is the relationship between the people?

**4. Conversation with examiner (1–2 minutes)**
- Questions about the scene and related topics from your own life

### Useful Phrases — Task 2

**Describing what you see:**
- En primer plano se ve...
- Al fondo aparece...
- A la izquierda/derecha hay...
- Parece que se trata de...

**Inferring:**
- Da la impresión de que...
- Parece como si + imperfect subjunctive
- Podría tratarse de...
- Imagino que momentos antes...

**Describing people's states:**
- Tiene cara de estar + adj
- Se les ve contentos / preocupados / cansados
- Por su gesto, diría que...

## Part 4: Task 3 — Role-play / Imagined Situation

### What You Receive
- A short scenario (often related to the Task 2 photo) and a role to play
- Example: "You are in a waiting room and start a conversation with the person next to you"

### Goals
- Open and sustain a conversation naturally
- React to what the examiner says
- Ask questions, make suggestions, reach some kind of resolution

### Useful Phrases — Task 3

**Opening a conversation:**
- Perdona, ¿te importa si...?
- Oye, ¿tú también estás esperando...?
- Disculpe, ¿sabe usted si...?

**Showing interest:**
- ¿Ah sí? Cuéntame más...
- Qué curioso, yo también...
- Pues no tenía ni idea...

**Making suggestions:**
- ¿Qué te parece si...?
- ¿Y si...?
- Podríamos...

**Wrapping up:**
- Bueno, ha sido un placer charlar contigo.
- Nos vemos, que tengo que...
- Si eso, quedamos otro día.

## Part 6: Preparation Strategy

### During Prep Time (15 minutes)

**Minutes 1–2: Read Task 1**
- Understand the situation and the proposals
- Pick 3 or 4 proposals you will prioritize

**Minutes 3–7: Plan Task 1**
- For each chosen proposal, note one clear reason
- Note 1–2 proposals you'd reject and why
- Anticipate follow-up questions

**Minutes 8–11: Plan Task 2**
- Pick your preferred photo (if there's a choice)
- Outline: setting, people, what's happening, what happens next
- Note useful vocabulary

**Minutes 12–15: Review**
- Mental rehearsal
- Breathe; think in Spanish

### Note-taking tips
- Keep notes in short bullet points, not full sentences — reading aloud kills fluency marks.
- Underline keywords you want to make sure to use.
- For Task 1, note a one-word "why" next to each proposal (e.g., *accesible*, *económico*, *urgente*).

## Part 7: Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Presentation
- Reading notes verbatim
- Memorizing a script (it sounds rigid; examiners notice)
- Speaking too fast; rushing to finish
- Ignoring part of what the prompt asks for

### Interaction
- One-word answers to follow-up questions
- Interrupting or not letting the examiner finish
- Getting defensive about your opinions — it's fine to change your mind with a reason

### Language
- Using only simple structures when the level expects variety
- Over-repeating the same connectors (*pero*, *y*, *también*)
- Mixing registers (tú/usted) inconsistently within a single task

---

## Part 8: Building Fluency

Speaking improves only with speaking. Practical approaches:

- **Record yourself** for 2–3 minutes on a random B2 topic, listen back, and note 3 things to fix next time. Repeat weekly.
- **Shadow native audio** — play a podcast and repeat along, matching rhythm and intonation.
- **Language exchange** (Tandem, HelloTalk, local meetups) for real interaction.
- **Structured mock exams** with a tutor or proficient speaker, ideally with written feedback.
