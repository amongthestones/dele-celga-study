# Module 1: Reading Strategies for DELE B2
## Comprehension, Cohesion, and Vocabulary in Context

---

## Overview

DELE B2 *Comprensión de Lectura* is 70 minutes with 4 tasks and 36 items. The tasks test the same skills in different formats — this module covers the strategies that work across all of them.

---

## Part 1: Understanding the B2 Reading Exam

### Exam Structure (4 tasks, 70 minutes, 36 items)

| Task | Type | Items |
|------|------|-------|
| 1 | Multiple choice (3 options) on one longer text | 6 |
| 2 | Match short statements to short texts (4 texts) | 10 |
| 3 | Sentence-insertion: place missing fragments back into a text | 6 |
| 4 | Cloze test: choose correct word for each gap (3 options) | 14 |

Always confirm the current format at cervantes.es — the exam specification is updated periodically.

### Text Types You'll Encounter
- Newspaper and magazine articles
- Opinion pieces and editorials
- Literary excerpts and personal narratives
- Informational and explanatory texts
- Biographical sketches, reviews, announcements

### What B2 Reading Tests
- Main ideas and supporting details
- Implicit meaning and author's attitude
- Text structure and paragraph-level cohesion
- Vocabulary in context (synonyms, register, collocations)
- Connectors and their logical function

## Part 2: Core Reading Strategies

### Strategy 1: Previewing (Vista Previa)

**Before reading the full text:**
1. Read the title and any subtitles
2. Look at any images, graphs, or captions
3. Read the first paragraph
4. Scan for names, dates, numbers
5. Note the source (newspaper, journal, etc.)

**Purpose:** Build expectations and activate background knowledge

### Strategy 2: Skimming (Lectura Rápida)

**Quick reading for general idea:**
1. Read first and last sentences of each paragraph
2. Pay attention to topic sentences
3. Don't stop at unknown words
4. Get the gist in 2-3 minutes

**Use for:** Getting overall understanding, deciding which sections to focus on

### Strategy 3: Scanning (Búsqueda de Información)

**Looking for specific information:**
1. Know what you're looking for before reading
2. Move eyes quickly over text
3. Stop when you find relevant information
4. Verify the information answers your question

**Use for:** Finding specific facts, names, numbers, dates

### Strategy 4: Intensive Reading (Lectura Intensiva)

**Careful, detailed reading:**
1. Read every word
2. Analyze sentence structure
3. Look up unknown vocabulary
4. Take notes
5. Reread difficult passages

**Use for:** Complex passages, answering detailed questions

### Strategy 5: Inference (Inferencia)

**Reading between the lines:**
1. What is NOT said explicitly?
2. What does the author assume?
3. What is the author's attitude?
4. What conclusions can be drawn?

**Key questions to ask:**
- Why did the author include this detail?
- What is the underlying message?
- What would the author agree/disagree with?

## Part 3: Identifying Text Structure

### Common Text Structures

**1. Chronological/Sequential**
- Signal words: primero, luego, después, finalmente, a continuación
- Use: Narratives, processes, historical accounts

**2. Cause and Effect**
- Signal words: porque, por lo tanto, como resultado, debido a
- Use: Explanatory texts, scientific articles

**3. Compare and Contrast**
- Signal words: sin embargo, mientras que, por el contrario, igualmente
- Use: Opinion pieces, analysis

**4. Problem and Solution**
- Signal words: el problema es, la solución, para resolver
- Use: News articles, policy discussions

**5. Classification/Categories**
- Signal words: se divide en, hay varios tipos, se clasifica
- Use: Informative texts, academic writing

**6. Argument and Support**
- Signal words: afirmo que, en primer lugar, además, en conclusión
- Use: Opinion essays, editorials

### Identifying the Author's Purpose

- **To inform:** Neutral tone, factual language
- **To persuade:** Opinion language, emotional appeals, strong arguments
- **To entertain:** Narrative elements, vivid language, personal anecdotes
- **To explain:** Step-by-step structure, definitions, examples

## Part 4: Understanding Implicit Meaning

### Types of Implicit Information

**1. Presuppositions**
What the author takes for granted:
- "Volvió a fracasar" presupposes previous failure
- "Dejó de fumar" presupposes he used to smoke

**2. Implications**
What can be logically concluded:
- "El restaurante siempre está lleno" implies it's popular/good

**3. Connotations**
Emotional associations of words:
- "político" vs "estadista" (same person, different connotation)
- "terco" vs "persistente" (same trait, different view)

**4. Tone and Register**
- Formal vs informal
- Serious vs humorous
- Critical vs supportive
- Objective vs subjective

### Practice: Identifying Attitude

Author attitudes to recognize:
- Crítico/a (critical)
- Favorable (favorable)
- Neutral/objetivo (neutral)
- Irónico/a (ironic)
- Escéptico/a (skeptical)
- Entusiasta (enthusiastic)
- Preocupado/a (concerned)
- Indiferente (indifferent)

**Clues to author attitude:**
- Word choice (positive vs negative connotations)
- Use of modifiers
- Rhetorical questions
- Exclamations
- Qualified statements

## Part 5: Vocabulary Strategies

### Strategy 1: Context Clues

**Types of context clues:**
1. **Definition:** The word is defined directly
   - "La inflación, es decir, el aumento general de precios..."

2. **Example:** Examples clarify meaning
   - "Frutas cítricas como naranjas, limones y pomelos..."

3. **Contrast:** Opposite meaning suggested
   - "No era tímido, sino más bien extrovertido."

4. **Synonym:** Similar word nearby
   - "El impedimento, el obstáculo principal..."

5. **General context:** Overall meaning helps
   - "Tras el terremoto, el pánico cundió entre la población."

### Strategy 2: Word Formation

**Prefixes:**
- des- (opposite): hacer → deshacer
- in-/im- (not): posible → imposible
- re- (again): leer → releer
- pre- (before): histórico → prehistórico

**Suffixes:**
- -ción/-sión (noun from verb): informar → información
- -miento (noun from verb): mover → movimiento
- -dad/-tad (abstract noun): igual → igualdad
- -mente (adverb): fácil → fácilmente
- -oso/-osa (full of): peligro → peligroso
- -able/-ible (able to be): comer → comestible

### Strategy 3: Word Families

If you know one word, you can often understand related words:
- económico → economía → economista → económicamente
- investigar → investigación → investigador → investigativo

## Part 6: Task-Specific Strategies

### Task 1: Multiple Choice on a Longer Text

**Strategy:**
1. Read questions before the text — know what you're looking for
2. Underline key words in questions
3. Read text with questions in mind; find the relevant section for each question
4. Eliminate clearly wrong answers first
5. Verify your choice against a specific line in the text

**Common traps:**
- Options using exact words from the text but with a wrong meaning
- Options that are true but don't answer the actual question
- Options that are too extreme (always/never) or too general

### Task 2: Matching Statements to Short Texts

**Strategy:**
1. Read all statements first; underline key concepts
2. Read the first short text and mark any statements that might match
3. Continue text-by-text
4. Revisit any uncertain matches at the end; use elimination

**Common traps:**
- Statements that are partially true of a text
- Similar vocabulary with different meaning
- True information that appears in more than one text but is specifically linked to only one

### Task 3: Sentence-Insertion

**Strategy:**
1. Read the whole text with the gaps as-is to get the overall argument
2. Look at the extracted sentences — what connectors or pronouns do they contain?
3. For each gap, ask: what comes before, what comes after, which sentence bridges them?
4. Trust cohesion signals: *este*, *por ello*, *sin embargo*, *además* all point backwards or forwards
5. One or two fragments are distractors — accept that not everything fits

**Tips:**
- Pronouns and demonstratives anchor sentences to a specific antecedent
- A sentence starting with a connector tells you its logical relationship to what comes before

### Task 4: Cloze / Gap-Fill

**Strategy:**
1. Read the whole text first for general understanding — don't start filling immediately
2. For each gap, consider grammar (verb tense, agreement, preposition) *and* meaning
3. Cross off options that are grammatically impossible before choosing on meaning
4. Check connectors against the logic of the paragraph
5. Review all gaps together for overall consistency

**Focus on:**
- Verb forms and tenses
- Pronouns and what they refer to
- Connectors and transitions
- Collocations (verb + noun pairs, preposition + noun)

## Part 7: Time Management

### Recommended Approach

1. **Quick preview** (2 min): Scan all tasks
2. **Task order:** Start with your strongest task type
3. **Don't get stuck:** Move on if struggling
4. **Mark uncertain answers:** Return later
5. **Transfer answers carefully:** Don't rush at the end

### Time Allocation (suggested)

| Activity | Time |
|----------|------|
| Preview all tasks | 2 min |
| Task 1 | 15 min |
| Task 2 | 15 min |
| Task 3 | 15 min |
| Task 4 | 15 min |
| Review and transfer answers | 8 min |
| **Total** | **70 min** |

## Practice Text Analysis

### Sample Analysis Framework

When analyzing any practice text, ask:

1. **What is the text type?** (article, essay, report, etc.)
2. **What is the topic?** (main subject)
3. **What is the author's purpose?** (inform, persuade, etc.)
4. **What is the author's attitude?** (positive, critical, neutral)
5. **How is the text structured?** (chronological, compare/contrast, etc.)
6. **What are the main arguments/points?**
7. **What evidence/examples are used?**
8. **What vocabulary requires attention?**
9. **What can be inferred (not stated directly)?**
