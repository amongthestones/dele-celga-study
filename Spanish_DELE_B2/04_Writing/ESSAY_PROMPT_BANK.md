## Formal Letters (FL)

### 1. [ ] Queja sobre transporte público
**Text type:** Formal complaint letter | **Words:** 150–180

You live in a city whose public bus service has recently cut routes and suffers frequent delays. Write a formal letter to the municipal transport authority. Cover:
- The specific impact on residents (elderly, those without cars)
- Your request for routes to be restored
- A proposal for a better complaints system

---

### 2. [ ] Solicitud de beca
**Text type:** Formal request letter | **Words:** 150–180

You have seen a notice for a grant supporting language and culture projects. Write a formal letter to the grants committee requesting funding. Cover:
- Your background and qualifications
- The specific project you would develop
- Why this project deserves support

---

### 3. [ ] Propuesta a un periódico local
**Text type:** Formal proposal letter | **Words:** 150–180

A local newspaper has announced it is cutting its arts and culture section. Write a formal letter to the editor proposing an alternative. Cover:
- Why cultural journalism matters in the community
- Your specific alternative proposal (digital-only, community contributions, partnerships)
- What you ask the newspaper to do

---

### 4. [ ] Reclamación a una plataforma digital
**Text type:** Formal complaint letter | **Words:** 150–180

After paying an annual subscription to an online learning platform, your account was blocked and customer service has been unhelpful. Write a formal complaint demanding resolution. Cover:
- The timeline of events and your attempts to resolve it
- The financial and practical impact
- The resolution you expect and by when

---

### 5. [ ] Carta a la dirección de un hospital
**Text type:** Formal letter | **Words:** 150–180

After a recent hospital visit you observed understaffing that affected care quality. Write a formal letter to the hospital director. Cover:
- Your specific observations (without identifying individuals)
- The systemic issue you believe caused the problem
- Concrete recommendations for improvement

---

### 6. [ ] Respuesta a una oferta de empleo
**Text type:** Formal application letter | **Words:** 150–180

An organization is looking for a project coordinator. Write a formal application letter. Cover:
- Your relevant experience and skills
- Why you are interested in the role
- What you would bring to the team

---

### 7. [ ] Propuesta a un ayuntamiento
**Text type:** Formal proposal letter | **Words:** 150–180

Your neighbourhood lacks green space. Write a formal letter to the town hall proposing the conversion of an abandoned plot into a community garden. Cover:
- The current problem and its impact on residents
- Your specific proposal with practical details
- How the town hall could support the initiative

---

### 8. [ ] Queja sobre publicidad engañosa
**Text type:** Formal complaint letter | **Words:** 150–180

You bought a product based on claims the product does not meet, and the company has not responded. Write a complaint to the consumer protection agency. Cover:
- The specific misleading claims
- Your attempts to resolve it with the company
- The action you expect from the agency

---

### 9. [ ] Carta a una editorial
**Text type:** Formal letter | **Words:** 150–180

You have finished a collection of short stories. Write a formal letter to a Spanish-language publisher submitting your work. Cover:
- A brief, compelling description of the collection
- Your target readership
- Why this publisher is the right fit

---

### 10. [ ] Propuesta de colaboración
**Text type:** Formal proposal letter | **Words:** 150–180

You represent a language school wanting to propose a collaboration with a local university's Spanish department. Write to the department head. Cover:
- What the collaboration would involve
- The benefit to students of both institutions
- The first concrete step you propose

---

### 11. [ ] Carta de apoyo a una causa
**Text type:** Formal letter | **Words:** 150–180

A community library is being closed due to budget cuts. Write to the regional culture department as a private citizen opposing the closure. Cover:
- The library's role in the community
- The disproportionate impact on vulnerable groups
- Alternatives to closure

---

### 12. [ ] Queja sobre condiciones laborales
**Text type:** Formal complaint letter | **Words:** 150–180

You have experienced repeated violations of agreed working conditions and internal complaints have been ignored. Write to the labour inspectorate. Cover:
- A factual account of the violations
- The impact on you and colleagues
- The outcome you are requesting

---

### 13. [ ] Solicitud de información sobre una ayuda pública
**Text type:** Formal request letter | **Words:** 150–180

You have read about a public subsidy but the official information is unclear and contradictory. Write to the responsible ministry requesting clarification. Cover:
- Specific questions you need answered
- The purpose of the request
- A reasonable response timeframe

---

### 14. [ ] Propuesta para un programa de radio
**Text type:** Formal proposal letter | **Words:** 150–180

A public radio station is accepting proposals for new cultural programming. Write pitching an original programme. Cover:
- The concept and format
- Why it meets a gap in current programming
- Your credentials or team

---

### 15. [ ] Respuesta a una organización profesional
**Text type:** Formal response letter | **Words:** 150–180

A professional association has asked you to justify missing a mandatory meeting that had administrative consequences. Respond formally. Cover:
- A clear, factual explanation
- An acknowledgment of the impact
- Your proposed next steps

## Opinion Essays and Articles (OE)

### 16. [ ] La inteligencia artificial en la educación
**Text type:** Opinion article | **Words:** 150–180

*"La inteligencia artificial en las aulas es una amenaza para el pensamiento crítico."*

Respond to this statement. You may agree, disagree, or take a nuanced position. Present two clear arguments and reach a defined conclusion.

---

### 17. [ ] El trabajo en remoto y la calidad de vida
**Text type:** Opinion essay | **Words:** 150–180

Has remote work genuinely improved quality of life for workers, or has it created new forms of exploitation and isolation? Take a clear position with supporting arguments.

---

### 18. [ ] La gentrificación de las ciudades
**Text type:** Magazine article | **Words:** 150–180

Write an article on the gentrification of city centres: who benefits, who is displaced, and what policy responses (if any) work.

---

### 19. [ ] Redes sociales y salud mental
**Text type:** Opinion article | **Words:** 150–180

*"Las plataformas digitales son las principales responsables de la crisis de salud mental entre los jóvenes."*

Respond with a developed argument. Consider platform design, parental responsibility, schools, individual agency.

---

### 20. [ ] El multilingüismo en Europa
**Text type:** Opinion essay | **Words:** 150–180

Does the European Union invest enough in promoting multilingualism among its citizens? Argue for or against, with concrete examples.

---

### 21. [ ] ¿Economía circular o marketing verde?
**Text type:** Magazine article | **Words:** 150–180

Is the "circular economy" a genuine shift in how we produce and consume, or primarily a marketing tool? Argue your view.

---

### 22. [ ] Los medios públicos en la era del streaming
**Text type:** Opinion essay | **Words:** 150–180

Is public broadcasting still necessary in an era of streaming services and social media? Defend your position.

---

### 23. [ ] Turismo masivo y patrimonio
**Text type:** Magazine article | **Words:** 150–180

Examine the tension between mass tourism and the preservation of cultural heritage in historic cities. Which regulatory models actually work?

---

### 24. [ ] La semana laboral de cuatro días
**Text type:** Opinion article | **Words:** 150–180

Several countries have trialled a four-day working week. Defend or critique the model, drawing on evidence from those trials.

---

### 25. [ ] Lenguas regionales: ¿protección o imposición?
**Text type:** Opinion essay | **Words:** 150–180

Regional language policies are defended as cultural protection and criticized as imposition. Write a nuanced opinion essay.

---

### 26. [ ] La meritocracia como mito
**Text type:** Magazine article | **Words:** 150–180

*"El éxito es el resultado del esfuerzo individual."* Is meritocracy a genuine principle or a social myth? What are the policy implications?

---

### 27. [ ] El envejecimiento de la población
**Text type:** Opinion essay | **Words:** 150–180

Many European countries face rapid population ageing. Is the current welfare-state model sustainable, and what should change?

---

### 28. [ ] La cultura de la cancelación
**Text type:** Opinion article | **Words:** 150–180

Is "cancel culture" legitimate social accountability or a form of censorship that harms public discourse? Take a defended position.

---

### 29. [ ] Alimentación y responsabilidad individual
**Text type:** Magazine article | **Words:** 150–180

Can individual dietary choices (e.g., reducing meat consumption) meaningfully affect the environment, or is systemic change the only effective lever?

---

### 30. [ ] La literatura en la sociedad contemporánea
**Text type:** Opinion essay | **Words:** 150–180

Amid podcasts, short videos, and social media, does literature still play an irreplaceable role? Defend your position.

---

## Reviews (RE)

### 31. [ ] Reseña de una novela
**Text type:** Review for a literary magazine | **Words:** 150–180

Write a review of a Spanish-language novel you have read. Cover: a brief plot summary (no spoilers), narrative style, themes, strengths and weaknesses, and the reader you would recommend it to.

---

### 32. [ ] Reseña de una película
**Text type:** Film review | **Words:** 150–180

Review a Spanish-language film. Cover: director and context, plot summary, cinematography/acting, themes, overall assessment.

---

### 33. [ ] Reseña de una exposición
**Text type:** Review for a cultural magazine | **Words:** 150–180

Review an art exhibition (real or imagined). Cover: the artist or theme, what the exhibition contained, what worked and what didn't, and who you would recommend it to.

---

### 34. [ ] Reseña de un podcast
**Text type:** Review for a media magazine | **Words:** 150–180

Review a Spanish-language podcast. Cover: format and concept, quality of presentation, depth of content, target audience, and whether it achieves its stated purpose.

---

### 35. [ ] Reseña de un festival cultural
**Text type:** Review | **Words:** 150–180

Review a cultural festival (music, literature, cinema, gastronomy). Cover: what it offered, atmosphere and organization, highlights, and whether you would recommend it.

---

## Semi-formal / Blog / Forum (SF)

### 36. [ ] Entrada de blog: viajes fuera de temporada
**Text type:** Blog post | **Words:** 150–180

Write a blog post for a travel site arguing that travelling off-season is a better experience than peak season. Use personal anecdotes, a friendly register, and direct appeals to the reader.

---

### 37. [ ] Mensaje en un foro sobre aprender idiomas
**Text type:** Forum message | **Words:** 150–180

On a language-learning forum, a beginner asks: *"¿Cuál es la mejor manera de aprender un idioma siendo adulto?"* Write a reply drawing on your experience. Friendly register; give two or three concrete pieces of advice.

---

### 38. [ ] Entrada de blog: trabajar y viajar
**Text type:** Blog post | **Words:** 150–180

Write a blog post about the realities (good and bad) of combining work and travel as a digital nomad. Use a personal, conversational tone and end with a reflection.

---

### 39. [ ] Mensaje en una red social de un barrio
**Text type:** Neighbourhood forum post | **Words:** 150–180

On a neighbourhood group, propose organising a monthly book swap in a local park. Persuade neighbours to participate. Semi-formal register; give practical details (date, place, how it works).

---

### 40. [ ] Entrada de blog: tecnología y niños
**Text type:** Blog post for a family-oriented site | **Words:** 150–180

Write a blog post aimed at parents about the role of screens in children's lives. Draw on personal or observed experience. End with a recommendation or reflection.
