# Module 1: DELE B2 Listening Strategies
## Audio Comprehension for DELE B2

---

## Overview

DELE B2 *Comprensión Auditiva* is 40 minutes with 5 tasks and 30 items. The audios range from short informal exchanges to semi-formal monologues.

---

## Part 1: Exam Structure (5 tasks, 40 minutes, 30 items)

| Task | Type | Plays | Items | Audio Type |
|------|------|-------|-------|------------|
| 1 | Multiple choice on short messages | 2x | 6 | Six short monologues (voicemail, announcement) |
| 2 | Multiple choice on a conversation | 2x | 6 | Informal conversation between two speakers |
| 3 | Multiple choice on an interview | 2x | 6 | Semi-formal interview |
| 4 | Match statements to speakers | 2x | 6 | Six short monologues on a shared theme |
| 5 | Multiple choice on a monologue | 2x | 6 | Semi-formal monologue (presentation, speech) |

Always confirm the current format at cervantes.es — the exam specification is updated periodically.

### What B2 Listening Tests
- Main ideas and specific details
- Speaker attitude and opinion
- Following the structure of an argument
- Basic implicit meaning and intent
- Differentiating between speakers in a conversation

## Part 2: Core Listening Strategies

### Strategy 1: Prediction (Predicción)

**Before listening:**
1. Read the questions carefully
2. Underline key words
3. Predict possible answers
4. Think about related vocabulary
5. Consider the topic and context

**Benefits:**
- Activates relevant vocabulary
- Focuses attention
- Makes listening more active

### Strategy 2: First vs Second Listening

**First listening:**
- Get the general idea
- Don't panic if you miss things
- Focus on main points
- Note which questions you can answer
- Mark uncertain answers

**Second listening:**
- Target unanswered questions
- Confirm your answers
- Listen for specific details
- Pay attention to nuances

### Strategy 3: Note-Taking

**Effective note-taking:**
- Use abbreviations
- Write in Spanish or your native language
- Focus on key words
- Note numbers, names, dates
- Mark opinion vs fact
- Use symbols: +, -, ?, !, →

**Common abbreviations:**
- xq = porque
- tb = también
- x = por
- q = que
- ej = ejemplo
- → = leads to
- ≠ = different from

### Strategy 4: Recognizing Distractors

**Common distractor types:**
1. Information mentioned but doesn't answer the question
2. Partial truth (some words match but meaning differs)
3. Information from wrong speaker
4. Information contradicted later
5. Similar-sounding words

**Protection strategy:**
- Wait for full context
- Don't rush to answer
- Listen for qualifiers and contradictions

### Strategy 5: Following the Speaker's Discourse

**Track the speaker's path:**
1. Main point introduction
2. Supporting arguments
3. Examples and evidence
4. Transitions to new points
5. Conclusions and opinions

**Listen for:**
- Topic sentences
- Discourse markers
- Changes in tone
- Summary statements

## Part 3: Understanding Different Accents

### Spanish Accents You'll Hear

**Peninsular Spanish (Spain):**
- Distinction between /s/ and /θ/ (c, z)
- "Vosotros" form used
- Tendency to drop final -s in some regions

**Latin American Spanish:**
- "Seseo" (no distinction between s and c/z)
- "Ustedes" instead of "vosotros"
- Various regional features

**Common Variants:**
- Argentinian: "vos" instead of "tú", "ll" pronounced as "sh"
- Mexican: Clear pronunciation, slight sing-song intonation
- Caribbean: Dropped final consonants, aspirated /s/
- Colombian: Often considered "neutral" Spanish

### Adaptation Strategies

1. **Expose yourself** to different accents regularly
2. **Focus on context** when sounds are unclear
3. **Learn regional vocabulary** variations
4. **Practice with authentic materials** from different countries

## Part 4: Discourse Markers to Listen For

### Introducing Ideas
- Para empezar... / En primer lugar...
- El tema del que voy a hablar...
- Me gustaría comenzar por...
- Quiero destacar que...

### Adding Information
- Además... / También... / Asimismo...
- Por otra parte... / Por otro lado...
- Es más... / Incluso...
- Cabe añadir que...

### Contrasting
- Sin embargo... / No obstante...
- Aunque... / A pesar de que...
- Por el contrario...
- Ahora bien...

### Giving Examples
- Por ejemplo... / Así...
- Como muestra... / Un ejemplo de esto...
- En concreto... / Concretamente...

### Expressing Opinion
- En mi opinión... / A mi juicio...
- Desde mi punto de vista...
- Personalmente, creo que...
- Me parece que...

### Concluding
- En conclusión... / Para terminar...
- En definitiva... / En resumen...
- Finalmente... / Por último...

### Agreeing/Disagreeing
- Estoy de acuerdo... / Comparto la opinión...
- No estoy de acuerdo... / Discrepo...
- Tiene razón en que... pero...

## Part 5: Task-Specific Strategies

### Task 1: Short Messages (6 items)

**Characteristics:**
- Six independent short audios (voicemails, announcements, adverts)
- Each has its own multiple-choice question
- Quick reset between items

**Strategy:**
1. Read the question and options for each item before the audio starts
2. Listen for the one detail that the question asks about
3. Don't carry over confusion from one item to the next — each resets

### Task 2: Informal Conversation (6 items)

**Characteristics:**
- Two speakers, informal register
- Items may ask about either speaker or the interaction as a whole

**Strategy:**
1. Identify the two speakers quickly (voice, gender, role)
2. Track who says what — items often test which speaker holds a given view
3. Listen for agreements, disagreements, and changes of mind
4. Note attitude markers (*claro*, *pues sí*, *no sé yo...*)

### Task 3: Interview (6 items)

**Characteristics:**
- Interviewer plus one guest, semi-formal register
- Questions test the guest's opinions and specific information they give

**Strategy:**
1. Treat the interviewer's questions as a roadmap of the items
2. Listen for the guest's reformulations and qualifications
3. Watch for hedging language (*en cierto modo, hasta cierto punto*) — it often signals a nuanced answer

### Task 4: Match Statements to Speakers (6 items)

**Characteristics:**
- Six short monologues on a shared theme, plus distractor statements
- You match each monologue to the statement that best summarizes it

**Strategy:**
1. Read all statements before listening, underline the distinguishing idea in each
2. On the first pass, capture each speaker's main point in one or two words
3. On the second pass, verify your matches and resolve any uncertain ones
4. Some statements are distractors — not every statement is used

### Task 5: Semi-formal Monologue (6 items)

**Characteristics:**
- Single speaker (presentation, speech, guided talk)
- Structured with clear sections

**Strategy:**
1. Note the topic and the speaker's purpose in the first sentences
2. Follow the structure (intro, body, conclusion)
3. Track main arguments and supporting evidence
4. Listen for any explicit conclusions or recommendations

## Part 6: Common Difficulties and Solutions

### Problem 1: Missing the Beginning
**Solution:**
- Use the reading time wisely
- Be ready before audio starts
- First sentence often introduces topic

### Problem 2: Getting Lost
**Solution:**
- Don't panic
- Listen for the next discourse marker
- Focus on the current question
- Use second listening to recover

### Problem 3: Unknown Vocabulary
**Solution:**
- Use context to guess meaning
- Don't stop at unknown words
- Focus on words you DO understand
- Similar-sounding words may help

### Problem 4: Fast Speech
**Solution:**
- Practice with native-speed materials
- Focus on stressed words
- Don't try to catch every word
- Train your ear with podcasts at 1.25x speed

### Problem 5: Multiple Speakers
**Solution:**
- Note speaker identifiers (name, gender, voice)
- Track by position (first speaker, second speaker)
- Listen for "según X" or "como dice Y"

## Part 8: Building a Listening Routine

Daily contact with authentic Spanish does more for listening than any single study session. Rotate material types so you hear different accents, registers, and speeds. A practical mix:

- **Active listening:** 15–20 minutes with something slightly above your comfort level — take notes, summarize in Spanish afterwards.
- **Passive listening:** background Spanish (podcast, radio, TV) while doing other tasks — trains your ear and catches vocabulary in context.
- **One focused exam task per week:** a full DELE B2 listening task under timed conditions.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Preparation matters** — use reading time on the options wisely.
2. **Don't panic** — you hear everything twice.
3. **Focus on meaning**, not every word.
4. **Practise regularly** with a variety of accents and topics.
5. **Track speakers** in conversations and interviews — many items depend on who said what.
6. **Use discourse markers** as signposts — they tell you what's coming next.
